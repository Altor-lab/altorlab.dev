altor-vec vs hnswlib-wasm

Two browser-capable HNSW approaches with different packaging tradeoffs. This is not an apples-to-apples benchmark in every category: the tools solve different jobs. The right choice depends on whether your search problem belongs in the browser or in infrastructure.

Representative numbers only. Exact bundle size, latency, and memory usage vary with hardware, build flags, vector dimensions, index parameters, and whether embeddings are generated locally or remotely.

Where altor-vec wins

  • Smaller published footprint for browser delivery
  • Simpler story for frontend SEO/demo pages and product widgets
  • A package positioned explicitly around browser-native vector search

Where hnswlib-wasm wins

  • Teams already standardized on hnswlib APIs
  • Build pipelines that prefer hnswlib compatibility
  • Situations where you want to compare against a well-known ANN baseline

Decision guide

If both meet your recall target, bundle size and frontend ergonomics become the practical decision factors.

Honest takeaway: If you need ACLs, huge datasets, hybrid ranking, or continuous writes, a server-side or database-style tool usually wins. If you need a tiny browser bundle, offline support, and zero backend search cost, altor-vec is the more natural fit.
